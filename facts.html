AMAZING FACTS

  • Underrepresentation in STEM Fields: Despite women comprising approximately 43% of the global workforce, they account for only 28% of the STEM workforce. (Financial Times)
  • Gender Pay Gap in STEM: In China, women earn, on average, 13% less than their male counterparts in STEM fields. (Financial Times)
  • Leadership Positions: Women hold only 28% of leadership positions in the technology sector worldwide. Source: PitchBook)
  • Venture Capital Funding: Startups founded by women receive only 2.3% of total venture capital funding globally. (Source: PitchBook)
  • STEM Degree Attainment: In the United States, women earn 57% of all bachelor's degrees but only 19% in engineering and 18% in computer science. (Source: Eurostat)
  • Research Publications: Women account for 30% of authorship in scientific publications, with significant variation across disciplines. (Source: Eurostat)
  • STEM Employment: In the European Union, women represent 41% of the STEM workforce, with the highest representation in life sciences and the lowest in information and communication technologies. (Source: Eurostat)
  • Gender Bias in Hiring: Studies have shown that resumes with male names are more likely to receive callbacks for STEM positions than those with female names. (Source: Eurostat)
  • Retention Rates: Women are more likely to leave STEM careers than men, with 50% of women leaving within 10 years compared to 31% of men. (Source: Eurostat)
